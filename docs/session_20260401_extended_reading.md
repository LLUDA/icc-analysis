## New Al Brooks Insights

### 1. Most Breakouts Fail
> *"Most breakouts fail. Failures are especially common with trend channel line breakouts since they are an attempt to accelerate a trend and a trend tends to weaken over time."*

**ICC Validation:** ICC waits for correction = avoids failed breakouts

### 2. Spike and Channel (Trend Lifecycle)
> *"Spike and channel behavior happens to some extent every day... The start of the channel usually becomes the start of an incipient trading range."*

**ICC Application:**
- Spike = ICC Indication
- Channel = ICC Correction
- ICC = trade channel completion + breakout

### 3. Tests = ICC Correction
> *"A test means that the market is returning to an area of support or resistance... Traders often place trades based on the behavior at the test."*

**ICC = Corrections are tests of broken structure**

### 4. 80% of Reversals Fail
> *"When there is a strong bull trend, it will resist change; almost every attempt at a reversal will end up as a bull flag, and the trend will then resume."*

**ICC = Trades the 80% failure of reversals!**

### 5. Signal Bars (ICC Entry Confirmation)
> *"The single most important skill that a trader can develop is the ability to determine the times when there will be more buyers or sellers above or below the prior bar."*

**Brooks Signal Bar Checklist:**
- ✅ Bull body
- ✅ Small lower tail
- ✅ Close above prior bar's close
- ✅ Strong follow-through bar

### 6. Reversal Bars (ICC Entry Candle)
**Bull Reversal Bar:**
- Close above open
- Lower tail ~1/3 to 1/2 bar height
- Small/no upper tail
- Follow-through bar strong

### 7. Two-Bar Reversals
> *"Whenever the market tries to do something twice and fails, it usually then tries the opposite. This is why double tops and bottoms work..."*

**ICC Structure:** First bar = move, second bar = failed attempt, breakout = entry

## New Adam Grimes Insights

### 1. Measured Move Objective
> *"A rough profit target for the follow-through leg... assumes that the CD leg will approximately equal the length of the AB leg."*

**ICC TP:** CD ≈ AB = measured move

### 2. Three Pushes (WARNING!)
> *"Three pushes... appears as three drives to a new high or low after a extended trend. The pushes are usually symmetrical."*

**ICC Rule:** After 3 pushes = expect larger correction/reversal

### 3. Simple vs Complex Pullbacks (Alternation)
> *"It is extremely unusual to find a trend that has five legs interrupted by four simple pullbacks; far more common would be two simple and two complex pullbacks."*

**ICC Application:**
- Pullbacks alternate: simple → complex → simple
- Use to anticipate next pullback type

### 4. Pullback Failure Patterns (ICC Exit Rules)
> *"Pullback trades fail in one of three ways:
> 1. No momentum out of pullback → flat range
> 2. Sharp countertrend momentum → pullback violated
> 3. Next leg fails at previous swing extreme"*

**ICC Exit Rules:**
1. No momentum → exit
2. Sharp violation → stop out
3. Failed at extreme → partial profits

### 5. Retracement Percentage
> *"A good rule of thumb is to expect retracements to terminate at about 50 percent of the preceding setup leg, plus or minus a very large margin of error."*

**ICC Filter:** 25-75% retracement = normal zone

### 6. Lower Time Frame Pullbacks
> *"Lower time frame ranges should be smaller in most pullbacks."*

**ICC:** Wait for LTF confirmation that correction is complete

### 7. Profit Targets
> *"The pivot high or low of the setup leg is an ideal target and should be considered the first, most conservative profit target."*

**ICC TP Hierarchy:**
1. Previous swing extreme (first/conservative)
2. Next structure level (secondary)
3. Trailing stop after 1:1
